Lesson 1: Describing comparing.

  1. Tall
    My brother is so tall that he has to look down to talk to my father.
    Giraffes are taller than elephants, but elephants are heavier.

  2. Short
    The table is short, making it easy for the children to reach.
    The chair is shorter than the table, making it easier for kids to sit on.

  3. Big
    Their dog is very big so he sleeps in a doghouse in the backyard.
    A blue whale is bigger than a shark.

  4. Small
    This is a small book, so it fits perfectly in my pocket.
    Ants are much smaller than butterflies, but they can carry heavy loads.

  5. Fast
    The car is very fast, and it overtakes all the other vehicles on the highway.
    Cheetahs are faster than lions, making them the fastest land animals.

  6. Slow
    The bus is slow today, taking much longer than usual to reach the next stop.
    Turtles are slower than rabbits, but they are known for their persistence.

  7. Old
    My house is old, with creaky floors and antique furniture.
    This castle is older than the museum, having stood for centuries.

  8. New
    She bought a new phone that has the latest features and a shiny screen.
    Her phone is newer than mine, and it has more advanced features.

  9. Hot
    The soup is hot, so be careful not to burn your tongue.
    The desert is hotter than the forest, especially during the daytime.

  10. Cold
    The water is cold, making it refreshing to drink on a hot day.
    The Arctic is colder than the Alps, with temperatures dropping below freezing.

  11. Heavy
    This bag is heavy, so I need help carrying it up the stairs.
    An elephant is heavier than a horse, but both are strong animals.

  12. Light
    The box is so light even a child can lift it with ease.
    Feathers are lighter than leaves so they will float farther distances in the air.

  13. Happy
    She feels happy today because she received good news from her friend.
    She is happier than her brother, especially after winning the prize.

  14. Sad
    He looks sad after hearing about the loss of his pet.
    He was sadder than usual after hearing the bad news.

  15. Expensive
    That watch is expensive, costing more than a month's salary.
    This car is more expensive than my house, showing its luxury.

  16. Cheap
    These shoes are cheap, but they are still very comfortable.
    These shoes are cheaper than my last pair, but they are still comfortable.

  17. Clean
    The room is clean, with no dust or clutter anywhere.
    The kitchen is cleaner than the living room, thanks to regular cleaning.

  18. Dirty
    Your shirt is dirty after playing in the mud all afternoon.
    His clothes are dirtier than mine after playing in the mud.

  19. Beautiful
    The park is beautiful, with colorful flowers and well-kept lawns.
    A colorful sunny spring day is more beautiful than a dull gray rainy day.

  20. Ugly
    That building is ugly, with its broken windows and peeling paint.
    That old building is uglier than the new one next to it.

  21. Easy
    The test was easy, and I finished it in just 10 minutes.
    This puzzle is easier than the one we did yesterday.

  22. Difficult
    Math is difficult for me, especially when it comes to algebra.
    The math exam was more difficult than the science test.

  23. Near
    The store is near my house, just a five-minute walk away.
    The bakery is nearer to my house than the supermarket.

  24. Far
    The beach is far from here, requiring a two-hour drive to reach.
    The beach is farther from our home than the mountains.

  25. Quiet
    The library is quiet, perfect for studying and reading.
    The library is quieter than the café, making it a better place to study.

  26. Noisy
    The street is noisy, with cars honking and people talking loudly.
    With constant traffic sounds, the city is noisier than the countryside.

  27. Thin
    She is thin, with a delicate frame and slender arms.
    My new phone is thinner than my old one, so it fits better in my pocket.

  28. Thick
    The book is thick, with over 500 pages to read.
    A blanket is much thicker than a bed sheet.

  29. Long
    Her hair is long, reaching all the way down to her waist.
    Her hair is longer than her sister's, reaching down to her waist.

  30. Short
    He has short hair, cut close to his scalp for a neat appearance.
    His speech was shorter than expected, but it covered all the main points.

Additional Sentences

Lesson 2: Shapes

1. Circle

A circle is round and has no corners or edges.

The pizza is shaped like a circle, but it's put in a square box for delivery.


2. Square

A square has four equal sides and four right angles.

The table is a square, so it fits perfectly in the corner of the room.


3. Rectangle

A rectangle has two long sides and two short sides.

The TV has a rectangular screen.


4. Triangle

A triangle has three sides and three corners.

The roof of the house is shaped like a triangle, pointing up to the sky.


5. Oval

An oval shape is similar to a stretched circle.

The egg is oval.


6. Diamond

A diamond shape looks like a square that has been turned on its side.

The kite is shaped like a diamond, flying high in the sky.


7. Star

A star shape has five or more points that extend outward.

The decoration on top of the Christmas tree is shaped like a star.


8. Cube

A cube has six equal square faces.

The dice we use to play games are shaped like cubes.


9. Sphere

A sphere is perfectly round, like a basketball.

The Earth is shaped like a sphere, which is why it has no edges.


10. Cylinder

A cylinder has two circular faces and one curved surface.

A can of soda is a cylinder, making it easy to hold and drink from.


11. Cone

A cone has a circular base and comes to a point at the top.

An ice cream cone is shaped like a cone, holding the ice cream inside.


12. Pyramid

A pyramid has a square base and triangular sides that meet at a point.

The Great Pyramid of Giza is a famous example of a pyramid.


Additional Sentences

  1. The circle is a simple shape, but it appears in many places, such as wheels and coins.

  2. A square has equal sides, making it a common shape for windows and tiles.

  3. Rectangles are often seen in everyday objects, like books and doors.

  4. Triangles are strong shapes and are used in construction for stability.

  5. Ovals can be found in nature, such as in the shape of some fruits and leaves.

  6. Diamonds are often associated with beauty and luxury, especially in jewelry.

  7. Boxes, such as delivery packages, are cube shaped.

  8. Spheres roll smoothly, which is why we see them in sports like soccer and basketball.

  9. Cylinders are often used in everyday items, like pipes and cans.

  10. Cones are shaped like ice-cream cones.

  11. Pyramids were built in Egypt in ancient times.
