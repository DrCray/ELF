Lesson 4: Requesting Movement or Concessions

Key Phrases

Basic patterns:

  • Would you be able to [action]?
  • Is there any flexibility on [issue]?
  • Could you reconsider [request]?
  • Would you be open to [proposal]?
  • Is there any possibility to [action]?

Softened requests:

  • We were wondering if [request]
  • Would it be possible to [action]?
  • Could we explore whether [possibility]?
  • Is there room to move on [issue]?
  • Might there be any way to [action]?

Additional expressions:

  • Would you consider [proposal]?
  • Could we discuss [possibility]?
  • Would you be willing to [action]?
  • Is there any chance of [outcome]?

Example Sentences

  • Would you be able to extend the deadline by two weeks?
  • Is there any flexibility on pricing for larger orders?
  • Could you reconsider the payment terms?
  • Would you be open to adjusting the delivery schedule?
  • Is there any possibility to bring the delivery date forward?
  • We were wondering if you could include installation in the package.
  • Would it be possible to make an exception?
  • Could we explore whether there's room to move on the delivery date?
  • Is there room to move on this issue?
  • Would you consider extending the payment period by two weeks?
  • Could we discuss a possible exception to the standard process?
  • Would you be willing to consider a one-time adjustment?

Dialog: Payment Terms & Conditions

note:
Net 30 means the payment is due in full 30 days from the invoice date

Customer: Our position is that the current payment terms are difficult for us.

Supplier: Unfortunately, we're not able to change the terms at the moment.

Customer: Would you be open to extending the payment period by two weeks?

Supplier: We could consider that, provided payments are made on schedule going forward.

Customer: That works for us and would help us manage our cash flow.

Supplier: Then we're agreed on the revised payment schedule.

Variation:

Customer: Our view is that the current payment terms of Net 30 are quite difficult for us.

Supplier: Unfortunately, we cannot change the standard terms easily right now.

Customer: Would you consider extending the payment period by two weeks, to Net 45?

Supplier: We could agree to that, provided that future payments are made on time as agreed.

Customer: That suits us and would really help with managing our cash flow.

Supplier: Excellent. We are agreed on the new Net 45 terms.
