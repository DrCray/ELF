Lesson 8: Signaling Agreement, Progress, or Deadlock

Key Phrases

Confirming agreement:

  • So we're agreed on [point]
  • We're aligned on [decision]
  • Then it sounds like we're agreed
  • That's settled then
  • We're in agreement on [issue]

Summarizing progress:

  • It sounds like we're close on [issue]
  • We've made good progress on [topic]
  • So far, we've agreed on [points]
  • Let me summarize where we are
  • To recap our discussion

Signaling pause or impasse:

  • At this point, we may need to revisit this later
  • Perhaps we should take some time to review this
  • Let's circle back to this
  • We might need to pause here and reconsider
  • This may require further discussion

Additional expressions:

  • Great
  • Excellent
  • Good
  • That works
  • Understood
  • Let's move forward with [decision]
  • Let's proceed with [action]

Example Sentences

  • So we're agreed on faster delivery at the current price.
  • We're aligned on the revised timeline.
  • Then it sounds like we're agreed on the new payment schedule.
  • That's settled then - we'll proceed with the additional support.
  • We're in agreement on the quality standards.
  • It sounds like we're close on pricing.
  • We've made good progress on the delivery terms.
  • So far, we've agreed on timeline and payment structure.
  • Let me summarize where we are: delivery in 7 weeks, payment Net 45.
  • To recap our discussion, we've confirmed quality specs and delivery schedule.
  • At this point, we may need to revisit this later with more information.
  • Perhaps we should take some time to review this internally.
  • Let's circle back to this after consulting with our teams.
  • We might need to pause here and reconsider our options.
  • This may require further discussion with senior management.
  • Great. Let's move forward with that adjustment.
  • Excellent. We are agreed on the new Net 45 terms.
  • Good. Then we are agreed on the new timeline of 7 weeks.
  • Understood. Let's gather the necessary details now and revisit this decision soon.

Dialog: Contract Finalization

Buyer: Let me summarize where we are: 500 units at $22 each, delivery in six weeks, payment Net 30.

Seller: That's correct. So we're agreed on those terms.

Buyer: We've made good progress on the main points. The only remaining issue is the warranty period.

Seller: Good point. We can offer a standard 12-month warranty. Does that work?

Buyer: That works. Then it sounds like we're agreed on all terms.

Seller: Excellent. We'll document this and send you the formal contract by tomorrow.

Variation:

Manager: To recap our discussion, we've confirmed the project scope, timeline, and budget.

Client: Yes. It sounds like we're close on everything except the payment schedule.

Manager: We could split it into three installments: 40% upfront, 40% at midpoint, 20% on completion.

Client: That seems fair. So far, we've agreed on scope, timeline, budget, and payment schedule.

Manager: Great. We're in agreement on all major points. Let's proceed with drafting the agreement.

Client: Perfect. Thank you for a productive discussion.

Additional Practice Patterns

Opening Negotiations

  • Thank you for meeting with us today
  • We appreciate the opportunity to discuss this
  • Let's start by reviewing the main points
  • I'd like to begin by clarifying our position
  • Before we start, let me outline our priorities

Clarifying and Checking Understanding

  • Just to clarify, [restatement]
  • Let me make sure I understand correctly
  • So what you're saying is [paraphrase]
  • Can I confirm that [point]?
  • Do I understand correctly that [interpretation]?

Buying Time

  • Let me check on that and get back to you
  • We'd need to review that internally
  • I'd like to consult with my team before confirming
  • Can we revisit this point later?
  • Let me think about that for a moment

Transitioning Between Topics

  • Moving on to the next point
  • Let's discuss [topic] now
  • The next issue to address is
  • Can we turn to [subject]?
  • I'd like to shift focus to [topic]

Closing Negotiations

  • I think we've covered everything
  • Let's summarize what we've agreed on
  • To confirm our understanding
  • We'll document this and send it to you
  • Thank you for a productive discussion
  • We'll follow up with the formal agreement
