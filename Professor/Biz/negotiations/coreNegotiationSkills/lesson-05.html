Lesson 5: Responding to Proposals

Key Phrases

Partial acceptance:

  • That could work, but [condition / concern]
  • We're open to that, provided that [condition]
  • That sounds reasonable, if [condition]
  • That might be viable, provided [condition]

Soft rejection:

  • We see your point; however, [concern]
  • I understand your concern; however, [limitation]
  • That's a fair point, however, [constraint]
  • I appreciate your flexibility; however, [issue]

Counter-proposals:

  • Instead of X, what about Y?
  • Rather than [proposal], we could [alternative]
  • As a compromise, [suggestion]

Additional expressions:

  • That could be acceptable for us
  • That seems acceptable
  • That works for us
  • That makes sense
  • I understand the issue
  • I appreciate that

Example Sentences

  • That could work, but we would need approval.
  • We're open to that, provided the quality standards are maintained.
  • That sounds reasonable, if we can confirm the timeline.
  • That might be viable, provided consistency in defect rates is guaranteed across batches.
  • We see your point; however, we need more time to review this internally.
  • I understand your concern; however, this still raises concerns for us.
  • That's a fair point, however, we're constrained by budget limitations.
  • I appreciate your flexibility; however, this still raises concerns.
  • Instead of reducing the price, what about offering additional services?
  • Rather than extending the deadline, we could add resources to the project.
  • As a compromise, we could split the payment into two installments.
  • That could be acceptable for us, as long as the overall timeline remains the same.
  • That seems acceptable. It would help us meet our own internal deadlines.

Dialog: Equipment Rental Terms

Supplier: We can rent you the projectors for $200 per week with a four-week minimum.

Client: That sounds reasonable, if we can reduce the minimum to two weeks.

Supplier: We see your point; however, our standard minimum is four weeks for equipment rentals.

Client: Rather than a four-week minimum, we could commit to renting multiple units for two weeks.

Supplier: That could be acceptable for us. As a compromise, you can rent three projectors for two weeks at the weekly rate.

Client: That makes sense. We're aligned on three units for two weeks.

Variation:

Supplier: The conference room rental is $500 per day, including basic equipment.

Client: I appreciate your flexibility; however, we need catering included in that price.

Supplier: Instead of including catering, what about a 10% discount if you book our catering separately?

Client: That might be viable, provided the catering discount applies to our entire order.

Supplier: That works for us. So we're agreed on the room rental plus 10% off catering.

Client: Excellent. That's settled then.
