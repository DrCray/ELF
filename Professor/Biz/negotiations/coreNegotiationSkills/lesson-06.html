Lesson 6: Justifying a Position or Request

Key Phrases

Basic patterns:

  • The reason for this is [explanation]
  • Based on our experience, [justification]
  • This would allow us to [benefit / outcome]
  • This is important because [reason]
  • The reason for our concern is [explanation]

Additional expressions:

  • This request stems from [source / requirement]
  • Given [context], we need [requirement]
  • Due to [situation], we require [request]
  • This would help us [outcome]
  • This would significantly help us [action]

Example Sentences

  • The reason for this is our internal quality requirements.
  • Based on our experience, shorter lead times improve overall efficiency.
  • This would allow us to meet our production schedule.
  • This is important because we need to maintain consistency across all suppliers.
  • The reason for our concern is budget pressure this quarter.
  • This request stems from our strict internal quality requirements for end-product reliability.
  • Given current market conditions, we need more flexible payment terms.
  • Due to resource limitations, we require a longer implementation period.
  • This would help us manage our cash flow.
  • This would help us meet our internal deadlines.
  • This would significantly help us manage the current transition period.
  • This would help us manage the situation better.

Dialog: Training Schedule Request

Employee: We need to extend the training program from two weeks to three weeks.

Manager: I understand. However, that would create scheduling challenges for us.

Employee: The reason for this request is that our team needs more time to practice the new software.

Manager: Based on our experience, most teams complete the training in two weeks. What specific areas need more time?

Employee: This would allow us to practice the advanced features thoroughly. Given our limited technical background, we need the extra week.

Manager: That makes sense. Let's extend it to three weeks with focused practice sessions in week three.

Variation:

Client: We're requesting a two-month extension on the project deadline.

Contractor: I understand your concern. However, extending by two months would affect our other commitments.

Client: This request stems from unexpected regulatory requirements we must complete first.

Contractor: This is important because it impacts our resource planning. Due to our current schedule, we can only offer a one-month extension.

Client: This would help us meet the regulatory deadline and still complete the project properly.

Contractor: Agreed. One month extension it is, and we'll adjust our resources accordingly.
