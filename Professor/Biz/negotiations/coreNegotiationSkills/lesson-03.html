Lesson 3: Making Proposals and Offers

Key Phrases

Basic patterns:

  • We could offer [solution]
  • What we can do is [action]
  • If you're open to X, we could consider Y
  • As an alternative, [proposal]
  • We'd be willing to [action]

Conditional offers:

  • If [condition], we could [action]
  • Provided that [condition], we would [action]
  • We might be able to [action], if [condition]
  • We could consider [action], as long as [condition]

Additional expressions:

  • One option would be to [action]
  • We're prepared to [action]
  • Instead, we could [alternative]
  • Would it help if we [action]?
  • We'd be prepared to propose [solution]

Example Sentences

  • We could offer additional support during the final phase.
  • What we can do is extend the warranty period by six months.
  • If you're open to a longer timeline, we could consider reducing the cost.
  • As an alternative, we could include extra user support and training at no additional cost.
  • We'd be willing to explore alternative specifications.
  • If delivery is flexible, we could offer a volume discount.
  • Provided that payments are made on schedule, we would extend the payment period.
  • We might be able to adjust the scope, if the budget allows.
  • We could consider that, as long as the overall timeline stays the same.
  • One option would be to phase the delivery over two months.
  • Instead, we could include additional support during the launch phase.
  • We're prepared to propose alternative specifications that could still satisfy your core needs.

Dialog: Scope, Features, or Workload

Client: From our perspective, the project scope doesn't fully meet our needs.

Provider: I understand your point; however, expanding the scope would be difficult under the current agreement.

Client: Would it be possible to adjust the scope slightly?

Provider: As an alternative, we could include additional support during the final phase.

Client: That could work for us, provided the overall timeline stays the same.

Provider: That makes sense. Let's move forward with that adjustment.

Variation:

Client: From our side, the current project scope does not completely meet our needs. We would like to add payment processing.

Provider: I understand your concern. However, adding that feature would be difficult under the current fixed agreement and budget.

Client: Is it possible to make a small adjustment to the scope?

Provider: Instead, we could include extra user support and training during the launch phase at no additional cost.

Client: That could be acceptable for us, as long as the overall timeline remains the same.

Provider: That works. Let's proceed with the additional support included.
