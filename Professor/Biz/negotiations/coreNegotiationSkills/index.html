Welcome

This course focuses on the essential language skills needed for effective business negotiations. Each lesson covers a specific negotiation task with key phrases, example sentences, and practice dialogs.

Course Structure

The course consists of 9 lessons. Lessons 1-8 focus on individual core negotiation skills, and Lesson 9 provides an extended practice dialog that integrates all skills:

  • Lesson 1: Stating a Position

    Learn how to clearly state your position and priorities in a negotiation.

  • Lesson 2: Identifying Constraints and Limits

    Express limitations and boundaries in a professional, diplomatic way.

  • Lesson 3: Making Proposals and Offers

    Present solutions and alternatives to move negotiations forward.

  • Lesson 4: Requesting Movement or Concessions

    Politely ask for flexibility or changes from the other party.

  • Lesson 5: Responding to Proposals

    Accept, reject, or modify proposals while maintaining positive relationships.

  • Lesson 6: Justifying a Position or Request

    Provide clear reasoning and support for your positions and requests.

  • Lesson 7: Managing Disagreement and Tension

    Handle conflicts professionally and keep negotiations productive.

  • Lesson 8: Signaling Agreement, Progress, or Deadlock

    Confirm decisions, summarize progress, and manage the negotiation process.

  • Lesson 9: Complete Negotiation Practice

    Practice all eight skills together in an extended, realistic negotiation dialog.

How to Use This Guide

Each lesson includes:

  • Key Phrases: Essential expressions organized by pattern type
  • Example Sentences: Practical examples showing phrases in context
  • Practice Dialogs: Complete conversations demonstrating the skills

Study the phrases, practice the examples, and use the dialogs to develop your negotiation language skills.
