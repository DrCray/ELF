Vocabulary

Entrepreneurs

  • People who start and run businesses.

  • Many entrepreneurs sell their products online these days.

  • Successful entrepreneurs often take risks to grow their companies.

Co-Founded

  • Started something with another person.

  • She co-founded a small bakery with her best friend.

  • They co-founded a charity to help children learn how to read.

Innovative

  • New and creative.

  • B1 Example*: The school uses innovative ways to teach math to kids.

  • His innovative ideas made the company more popular than ever.

Fascinated

  • Very interested in something.

  • My brother is fascinated by cars and watches videos about them.

  • She was fascinated by the history of ancient cities during her trip.

Impressed

  • Feeling admiration for someone or something.

  • The teacher was impressed with my homework because it was neat.

  • We were all impressed by how quickly she learned the new skill.

Initiative

  • Taking action without being told.

  • He showed initiative by cleaning the room before I asked.

  • Her initiative in solving the problem saved the team a lot of time.

Internship

  • A temporary job for experience.

  • She got an internship at a big company last summer.

  • His internship at the hospital taught him a lot about medicine.

Opportunities

  • Chances to do something valuable.

  • Living in a city gives you more opportunities to find a job.

  • Studying abroad offers opportunities to learn about new cultures.

Fund

  • Provide money for something.

  • My parents fund my school trips every year.

  • The government will fund the new park to make the town nicer.

Venture

  • A risky or daring project, often a business.

  • Starting a small shop was her first venture after college.

  • Their venture into selling handmade jewelry became very successful.

Kit

  • A set of parts to build something.

  • He bought a kit to make a model airplane at home.

  • The science kit came with everything needed for the experiment.

Primarily

  • Mainly or mostly.

  • I primarily use my phone to text my friends.

  • The club is primarily for people who love playing sports.

Generated

  • Produced or created something.

  • The sale generated enough money to buy new books.

  • Her speech generated a lot of excitement among the students.

Collaboration

  • Working together with someone.

  • Our collaboration on the project made it finish faster.

  • The collaboration between the two artists created a beautiful painting.

Crucial

  • Extremely important.

  • Water is crucial for plants to grow well.

  • His advice was crucial in helping me pass the difficult test.

Marketing

  • Promoting or selling products.

  • B1 Example*: Good marketing helps sell more ice cream in the summer.

  • She studied marketing to learn how to advertise her business.

Affordable

  • Reasonably priced for most people.

  • These shoes are affordable, so I can buy two pairs.

  • The new phone is affordable for students with small budgets.

Revolutionary

  • Completely new and changing things.

  • The revolutionary invention made cooking much easier.

  • His revolutionary ideas changed how people think about space travel.

Sleek

  • Smooth and stylish in design.

  • The new car has a sleek look that everyone likes.

  • She bought a sleek laptop that fits easily in her bag.

Features

  • Special characteristics or functions.

  • My camera has features like zoom and night mode.

  • The app’s new features make it more fun to use every day.

Risk

  • The possibility of something going wrong.

  • Climbing the mountain has a big risk, but it’s exciting.

  • Starting a business is a risk, but it could make you rich.

Turn It Into

  • Transform something into something else.

  • She wants to turn it into a game that everyone can play.

  • He worked hard to turn it into a successful online store.

Accessible

  • Easy for people to use or get.

  • The park is accessible by bus or car.

  • This website is accessible to people who can’t see well.
