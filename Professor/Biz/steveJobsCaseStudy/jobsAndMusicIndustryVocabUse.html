English Lesson
Jobs and the Music Industry, Vocabulary
  1. Launched: (Meaning: introduced or started something new)
    • The company launched a new line of eco-friendly clothing to appeal to environmentally conscious consumers.
  2. Crisis: (Meaning: a time of intense difficulty or danger)
    • During the economic crisis, many small businesses struggled to stay afloat due to declining sales.
  3. Illegal: (Meaning: against the law)
    • The website was shut down for hosting illegal content that violated copyright laws.
  4. Reduced: (Meaning: decreased in amount or extent)
    • The new recycling program reduced the amount of waste sent to landfills by 30%.
  5. Cautious: (Meaning: careful to avoid risks)
    • The investor was cautious about funding the startup until they presented a solid business plan.
  6. Transform: (Meaning: dramatically change or improve)
    • The new principal worked to transform the school by introducing innovative teaching methods.
  7. Profitable: (Meaning: generating financial gain)
    • The café became profitable after introducing a popular line of specialty drinks.
  8. Revenue: (Meaning: income generated from business activities)
    • The festival generated significant revenue through ticket sales and vendor fees.
  9. Distribution: (Meaning: the process of delivering products to consumers)
    • The company improved its distribution network to ensure faster delivery of online orders.
  10. Piracy: (Meaning: unauthorized use or reproduction of copyrighted material)
    • The film industry implemented stricter measures to combat piracy and protect their movies.
  11. Profits: (Meaning: financial gains after expenses)
    • The bookstore saw a rise in profits after hosting a series of popular author events.
  12. Executives: (Meaning: senior managers or leaders in a company)
    • The executives met to discuss the company’s strategy for entering the international market.
  13. Skeptical: (Meaning: doubtful or questioning)
    • She was skeptical about the new software’s ability to improve productivity until she saw it in action.
  14. Resistant: (Meaning: unwilling to accept or adopt something)
    • Some employees were resistant to the new remote work policy, preferring the traditional office setup.
  15. Proposal: (Meaning: a plan or suggestion put forward for consideration)
    • The team submitted a proposal to redesign the city park with more green spaces.
  16. Strategy: (Meaning: a plan to achieve a goal)
    • The marketing strategy focused on targeting younger audiences through social media campaigns.
  17. Aligning: (Meaning: bringing into agreement or cooperation)
    • The nonprofit worked on aligning its mission with the goals of its community partners.
  18. Interface: (Meaning: the point of interaction between a user and a system, often digital)
    • The app’s intuitive interface made it easy for users to navigate and book appointments.
  19. Encourage: (Meaning: to promote or support something)
    • The teacher used positive feedback to encourage students to participate in class discussions.
  20. Hinder: (Meaning: to create obstacles or prevent progress)
    • Poor internet connectivity could hinder the team’s ability to complete the project on time.
  21. Proposed: (Meaning: suggested or put forward for consideration)
    • The city council proposed a new bike lane system to improve urban mobility.
  22. Ensured: (Meaning: made certain that something would happen)
    • The event organizers ensured that all attendees had access to free transportation.
  23. Revenue streams: (Meaning: sources of income for a business)
    • The gym diversified its revenue streams by offering online fitness classes and merchandise.
  24. Promote: (Meaning: to actively support or advertise something)
    • The organization worked to promote recycling by distributing educational materials to schools.
  25. Sustainable: (Meaning: capable of being maintained over time)
    • The farmer adopted sustainable practices to protect the soil and ensure long-term productivity.
  26. Confidence: (Meaning: trust or belief in something’s reliability)
    • The successful pilot program gave the team confidence in rolling out the new initiative.
  27. Demonstrated: (Meaning: showed or proved through evidence)
    • The scientist demonstrated the effectiveness of the new vaccine through rigorous testing.
  28. Addressed concerns: (Meaning: responded to worries or objections)
    • The manager addressed concerns about the new schedule by explaining its benefits to the staff.
  29. Revenue-sharing: (Meaning: an agreement to divide income among partners)
    • The authors signed a revenue-sharing deal to split profits from their co-written book.
  30. Venture: (Meaning: a business project, often involving risk)
    • The entrepreneurs launched a new venture to develop affordable solar energy solutions.
  31. Restore: (Meaning: bring back to a previous or improved state)
    • The community worked together to restore the historic theater to its former glory.
  32. Profitability: (Meaning: the ability to generate profit)
    • The clothing brand achieved profitability by focusing on high-demand, trendy designs.
  33. Win-win: (Meaning: a situation where all parties benefit)
    • The partnership between the school and the local business was a win-win, providing students with internships and the company with fresh talent.
  34. Revolutionized: (Meaning: radically changed or transformed)
    • The introduction of online learning platforms revolutionized access to education worldwide.
  35. Consumption: (Meaning: the act of using or purchasing goods/services)
    • The campaign aimed to reduce energy consumption by encouraging households to use LED lights.
  36. Struggling: (Meaning: experiencing difficulty or challenges)
    • The struggling bookstore turned to crowdfunding to cover its operating costs.
  37. Positioned: (Meaning: placed in a particular role or status)
    • The startup positioned itself as a leader in sustainable packaging by using biodegradable materials.
  38. Innovation: (Meaning: the introduction of new ideas or methods)
    • The company’s commitment to innovation led to the development of a groundbreaking medical device.
  39. Resistance: (Meaning: opposition to change or new ideas)
    • The team faced resistance from the community when proposing a new wind farm project.
