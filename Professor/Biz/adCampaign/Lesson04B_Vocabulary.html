Lesson 4B: Business Vocabulary

CEFR A2+ Vocabulary from Scenario 4

This lesson covers 28 essential business vocabulary words used when planning promotional events and customer engagement strategies. Each word is defined and illustrated with an example sentence.

1. Discussion (noun)
Definition: A conversation or debate about a particular topic; the exchange of ideas.
Example: The team had a productive discussion about next quarter's goals.
2. Firsthand (adjective/adverb)
Definition: Directly from the original source; obtained or learned through personal experience.
Example: The manager witnessed the problem firsthand during her visit to the factory.
3. Crucial (adjective)
Definition: Extremely important or essential; decisive or critical.
Example: Customer feedback is crucial for improving our service quality.
4. Engaging (adjective)
Definition: Attracting and holding attention or interest; charming or appealing.
Example: The presenter gave an engaging talk that kept everyone's attention.
5. Dedicated (adjective)
Definition: Devoted or committed to a particular purpose or task; exclusively assigned.
Example: We set up a dedicated support line for premium customers.
6. Pop-up (adjective)
Definition: Temporary and appearing suddenly; a short-term retail or event space.
Example: The brand opened a pop-up store in the city center for the holiday season.
7. High-traffic (adjective)
Definition: Experiencing a large number of people passing through; busy or heavily used.
Example: The store is located in a high-traffic area near the subway station.
8. Demonstrate (verb)
Definition: To show or make evident through examples or practical application; to prove.
Example: The sales representative will demonstrate how the software works.
9. Transformative (adjective)
Definition: Causing a major change or improvement; having the power to dramatically alter something.
Example: The new technology had a transformative effect on the industry.
10. Knowledgeable (adjective)
Definition: Well-informed and intelligent; having extensive knowledge about a subject.
Example: The knowledgeable staff can answer any questions about our products.
11. On-site (adjective/adverb)
Definition: Located at or happening at the place being discussed; at the actual location.
Example: We have on-site technical support available during business hours.
12. Suitable (adjective)
Definition: Appropriate or right for a particular purpose, person, or occasion.
Example: This office space is suitable for a team of ten people.
13. Complimentary (adjective)
Definition: Given free as a courtesy or gift; provided without charge.
Example: Hotel guests receive complimentary breakfast each morning.
14. Analyze (verb)
Definition: To examine something in detail to understand it better or discover more about it.
Example: The team will analyze the sales data to identify trends.
15. Tailored (adjective)
Definition: Made or adapted for a particular purpose or person; customized.
Example: We offer tailored solutions to meet each client's specific needs.
16. Ambiance (noun)
Definition: The character and atmosphere of a place; the mood or feeling created by an environment.
Example: The restaurant has a romantic ambiance with soft lighting and quiet music.
17. Refreshments (noun)
Definition: Light snacks and drinks, especially those served at meetings or events.
Example: Refreshments will be served during the networking session.
18. Informational (adjective)
Definition: Providing useful information; educational or instructive.
Example: The website has an informational section about the company's history.
19. Informed (adjective)
Definition: Having knowledge about something; educated or aware of facts.
Example: Customers can make informed choices when they have all the necessary information.
20. Incentives (noun)
Definition: Something that motivates or encourages someone to do something; rewards or benefits offered.
Example: The company offers performance incentives to motivate sales staff.
21. Gift-with-purchase (noun phrase)
Definition: A free item given to customers when they buy a product; a promotional offer.
Example: Customers receive a tote bag as a gift-with-purchase when they spend over $50.
22. Urgency (noun)
Definition: The quality of being important and requiring immediate action or attention; pressing need.
Example: The limited-time offer creates a sense of urgency among shoppers.
23. Hashtags (noun)
Definition: Words or phrases preceded by the # symbol, used on social media to identify topics or themes.
Example: Use the hashtags #NewProduct and #Innovation when posting about the launch.
24. Efficacy (noun)
Definition: The ability to produce the desired result; effectiveness.
Example: Clinical trials demonstrated the efficacy of the new medication.
25. Organizing (verb)
Definition: Arranging or structuring something systematically; planning and coordinating an event or activity.
Example: The committee is organizing a charity fundraiser for next month.
26. Immersive (adjective)
Definition: Providing a completely engaging experience; deeply absorbing or involving.
Example: The virtual reality game offers an immersive experience for players.
27. Cosmetics (noun)
Definition: Products applied to the face and body to enhance appearance; makeup and beauty products.
Example: The cosmetics industry continues to grow with increasing demand worldwide.
28. Resistance (noun)
Definition: Unwillingness to accept change or new ideas; refusal to comply or opposition.
Example: The new software faced initial resistance from employees used to the old system.
1. Refer back
Definition: To look at or consult something again; to return to previously provided information or materials.
Example: If you have questions about the policy, please refer back to the employee handbook we distributed last month.
