Lesson 11B: Vocabulary

Business Vocabulary from Scenario 11 (B1+ Level)

This lesson presents important business vocabulary used in Scenario 11. Each word is defined and illustrated with an example sentence relevant to business contexts.

1. Ramifications
Definition: Consequences or effects of an action or decision, especially when complex or unwelcome.
Example: We need to carefully consider the ramifications of changing our pricing structure.
2. Credibility
Definition: The quality of being trusted and believed in; reliability and trustworthiness.
Example: The expert's endorsement added significant credibility to our research findings.
3. Embodies
Definition: To represent or express an idea, quality, or feeling in a tangible or visible form; to personify.
Example: Our CEO embodies the innovative spirit that drives our company forward.
4. Convey
Definition: To communicate or make known; to express or transmit an idea, feeling, or message.
Example: Our advertising campaign aims to convey a message of environmental responsibility.
5. Versatility
Definition: The ability to adapt or be used in many different ways; flexibility and multi-functionality.
Example: The product's versatility makes it suitable for both professional and personal use.
6. Dedicated
Definition: Devoted to a task or purpose; committed and focused on achieving something.
Example: Our dedicated team worked overtime to meet the project deadline.
7. Demographics
Definition: Statistical data about populations and particular groups within them, often used to identify market segments.
Example: We analyzed the demographics to determine which age groups prefer our product.
8. Acknowledge
Definition: To accept or recognize the truth or existence of something; to admit.
Example: The company was quick to acknowledge the mistake and offer compensation.
9. Integrity
Definition: The quality of being honest and having strong moral principles; adherence to ethical standards.
Example: Business integrity is essential for building long-term customer relationships.
10. Transparency
Definition: Openness and honesty in communication; the quality of being clear and easily understood.
Example: The organization values transparency in all its financial reporting.
11. Crucial
Definition: Extremely important or essential; of critical significance to the success of something.
Example: Employee training is crucial for maintaining high quality standards.
