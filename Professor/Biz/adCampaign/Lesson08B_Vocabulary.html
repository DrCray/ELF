Lesson 8B: Business Vocabulary

CEFR B1+ Vocabulary from Scenario 8

This lesson covers advanced business vocabulary words (B1 level and above) used when discussing ethical standards, regulatory compliance, and corporate social responsibility. Each word is defined and illustrated with an example sentence.

Representative (noun)
Definition: A person who acts or speaks on behalf of another person or organization.
Example: The celebrity's representative negotiated the endorsement contract.
Assured (adjective/past participle)
Definition: Confident and certain about something; guaranteed or convinced that something is true.
Example: How can we be assured that the company follows ethical labor practices?
Gravity (noun)
Definition: The seriousness or importance of a situation; weight or significance.
Example: We understand the gravity of these environmental concerns.
Adhere (verb)
Definition: To follow, obey, or stick to rules, standards, or guidelines.
Example: All suppliers must adhere to international labor standards.
Implement (verb)
Definition: To put a plan, system, or policy into action or effect.
Example: We will implement new safety measures next quarter.
Robust (adjective)
Definition: Strong, effective, and thorough; able to withstand difficulties.
Example: The company has robust quality control procedures.
Comply (verb)
Definition: To act in accordance with rules, regulations, or requests.
Example: All factories must comply with local environmental regulations.
Audits (noun)
Definition: Official examinations or inspections of records, processes, or accounts.
Example: Regular audits ensure compliance with our ethical standards.
Inspections (noun)
Definition: Careful examinations or checks to ensure standards are met.
Example: Surprise inspections help maintain workplace safety.
Verify (verb)
Definition: To confirm that something is true, accurate, or meets requirements.
Example: We verify all supplier certifications before signing contracts.
Reassuring (adjective)
Definition: Making someone feel less worried or uncertain; comforting.
Example: The manager's explanation was reassuring to the concerned investors.
Prioritize (verb)
Definition: To arrange or deal with in order of importance; to give priority to.
Example: We prioritize sustainable materials in our sourcing decisions.
Sustainable (adjective)
Definition: Able to be maintained or continued without depleting resources or harming the environment.
Example: The company uses only sustainable packaging materials.
Conservation (noun)
Definition: The careful use and protection of natural resources to prevent waste or depletion.
Example: Water conservation is critical in our manufacturing operations.
Commendable (adjective)
Definition: Deserving praise or recognition; admirable.
Example: Your team's efforts on this project are truly commendable.
Utilize (verb)
Definition: To make practical and effective use of something.
Example: We utilize advanced computer modeling to test product safety.
Empowerment (noun)
Definition: The process of giving people authority, confidence, or power to control their lives.
Example: Our programs focus on the empowerment of women in developing countries.
