Lesson 5B: Business Vocabulary

CEFR A2+ Vocabulary from Scenario 5

This lesson covers 33 essential business vocabulary words used when discussing celebrity endorsements and brand partnerships. Each word is defined and illustrated with an example sentence.

1. Leveraging (verb)
Definition: Using something to maximum advantage; making the best strategic use of a resource.
Example: The company is leveraging its brand reputation to enter new markets.
2. Significantly (adverb)
Definition: To a sufficiently great or important degree; notably or considerably.
Example: Sales have increased significantly since the new marketing campaign launched.
3. Visibility (noun)
Definition: The degree to which something attracts public attention; how easily something is seen or noticed.
Example: The sponsorship deal increased the brand's visibility among younger consumers.
4. Credibility (noun)
Definition: The quality of being trusted and believed in; reliability and trustworthiness.
Example: The expert's endorsement added credibility to the product claims.
5. Celebrities (noun)
Definition: Famous people, especially in entertainment or sports.
Example: Many celebrities attended the charity gala last night.
6. Respected (adjective)
Definition: Admired and valued for achievements, qualities, or position; held in high regard.
Example: She is a respected leader in the technology industry.
7. Endorsement (noun)
Definition: A public statement or action showing support or approval, especially of a product by a celebrity.
Example: The athlete's endorsement helped boost sales of the sports drink.
8. Worth considering (phrase)
Definition: Deserving of careful thought or attention; meriting consideration.
Example: The proposal is definitely worth considering given the potential benefits.
9. Radiant (adjective)
Definition: Glowing or shining brightly; appearing healthy, happy, and attractive.
Example: The bride looked radiant on her wedding day.
10. Resonates (verb)
Definition: Has particular meaning or importance for someone; appeals to or connects with.
Example: The company's mission statement resonates with employees who value social responsibility.
11. Reinforce (verb)
Definition: Strengthen or support an idea, feeling, or message; make stronger.
Example: The training program will reinforce the safety procedures we discussed.
12. Enhance (verb)
Definition: Improve the quality, value, or attractiveness of something; make better.
Example: The new lighting will enhance the appearance of the product displays.
13. Embodies (verb)
Definition: Represents or expresses something in a tangible or visible form; personifies.
Example: She embodies the values of dedication and professionalism.
14. Vitality (noun)
Definition: Energy, liveliness, and enthusiasm; the state of being strong and active.
Example: The new CEO brought vitality and fresh ideas to the company.
15. Admired (verb)
Definition: Regarded with respect, approval, or pleasure; looked up to.
Example: The mentor is widely admired for her leadership skills.
16. Age-defying (adjective)
Definition: Appearing younger than one's actual age; resisting the effects of aging.
Example: The actress's age-defying appearance has made her a popular spokeswoman for skincare products.
17. Remarkable (adjective)
Definition: Worthy of attention; extraordinary or exceptional.
Example: The company achieved remarkable growth in just two years.
18. Influence (noun)
Definition: The capacity to have an effect on someone's character, development, or behavior; power to affect.
Example: Social media influencers have significant influence over their followers' purchasing decisions.
19. Align (verb)
Definition: Be in correct or appropriate relative positions; match or correspond with.
Example: Our company values align with those of our business partners.
20. Global recognition (noun phrase)
Definition: Worldwide fame or acknowledgment; being known internationally.
Example: The brand has achieved global recognition through strategic marketing.
21. Relatable (adjective)
Definition: Easy to understand or identify with; able to be connected with personally.
Example: The speaker told relatable stories that resonated with the audience.
22. Aspirational (adjective)
Definition: Having or characterized by ambitions to achieve something; inspiring desire to emulate.
Example: The luxury brand uses aspirational marketing to attract customers.
23. Cultural backgrounds (noun phrase)
Definition: The customs, traditions, and social heritage of different groups or societies.
Example: The diverse team brings perspectives from various cultural backgrounds.
24. Inclusivity (noun)
Definition: The practice of including people who might otherwise be excluded or marginalized; welcoming diversity.
Example: The organization promotes inclusivity in all its hiring practices.
25. Massive (adjective)
Definition: Extremely large or substantial; huge in scale or extent.
Example: The concert attracted a massive crowd of over 50,000 people.
26. Engages (verb)
Definition: Occupies, attracts, or involves someone's interest or attention; participates or becomes involved.
Example: The interactive display engages customers and encourages them to explore products.
27. Exposure (noun)
Definition: Public attention or visibility; the state of being noticed or gaining experience.
Example: The media coverage gave the startup valuable exposure to potential investors.
28. Collaborations (noun)
Definition: Joint efforts or partnerships between individuals or organizations working together.
Example: The artist's collaborations with musicians have produced award-winning work.
29. Cross-promotions (noun)
Definition: Marketing strategies where two or more brands promote each other's products.
Example: The cross-promotions between the movie and the fast-food chain benefited both companies.
30. Undoubtedly (adverb)
Definition: Without doubt; certainly or definitely.
Example: The new product will undoubtedly appeal to our target demographic.
31. Elevate (verb)
Definition: Raise to a higher level; improve or enhance something to make it better.
Example: The partnership will elevate the brand's status in the luxury market.
32. Propel (verb)
Definition: Drive, push, or cause to move forward or continue; advance or promote.
Example: The successful campaign will propel the company to market leadership.
33. Industry (noun)
Definition: A particular area of business or commercial activity; a sector of the economy.
Example: She has worked in the technology industry for over fifteen years.
