Section 9: Using "Might" to Soften Statements

Using the verbs “will” or “is” in statements can sometimes come across as strong or even arrogant. By replacing “will” or “is” with “might,” you can soften the suggestion, making it more polite and considerate. Here are some strategies and examples to help you use "might" effectively.

Examples

1. Will to Might

Strong: We will leave if the director doesn’t show soon.

Polite: We might leave if the director doesn’t show soon.

2. Is to Might

Strong: It is necessary to build more factories.

Polite: It might be necessary to build more factories.
