LESSON 3

Infinitive Phrases

Main Page Lesson 1 Lesson 2 Lesson 3

Infinitive phrases use to + verb and can function as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. This lesson demonstrates the versatility of infinitive phrases in different sentence positions, showing how they express purpose, provide modification, or act as subjects and objects.

Infinitive Phrases in the DIRECT OBJECT slot
The manager decided to delay the launch last week.
She planned to review the proposal after the meeting.
They agreed to adjust the schedule this morning.
The company aims to reduce costs this year.
He promised to follow the procedure during training.
The team expects to finish the project on time.
Infinitive Phrases in the SUBJECT slot
To reduce costs gave management strong motivation during the review.
To expand the market required bold leadership in the early stages.
To master the violin demanded hours of practice every single evening.
To bake sourdough bread requires specific temperatures during the fermentation.
To learn a new language provides cognitive benefits throughout old age.
To increase productivity gave the project new importance this quarter.
Infinitive Phrases modifying the DIRECT OBJECT
The manager gave the team (time to review the proposal) yesterday.
She gave employees (a chance to ask questions) after the meeting.
They gave the client (an opportunity to respond) this morning.
The company gave staff (permission to work remotely) during repairs.
He gave the group (instructions to follow) during the exercise.
The trainer gave participants (tasks to complete) before lunch.
