Remarkable Intelligence of the Corvid Birds - Podcast

  • Host: I just read this wild article about crows and ravens, and now I feel kind of dumb, honestly.  
  • Guest: Why's that?  
  • Host: Because apparently calling someone a bird brain is basically an insult to actual birds. These things are geniuses.  
  • Guest: Oh, corvids? Yeah, I've read about this before. They're sharp as a tack.  
  • Host: Exactly. They actually make tools — bending twigs into hooks to fish bugs out of holes.  
  • Guest: That's not just instinct, that's basically on par with what chimps can do.  
  • Host: Right? And get this — if a raven decides it doesn't like you, it will hold a grudge for years.  
  • Guest: Okay, that's terrifying. I do not want a bird giving me the cold shoulder for the rest of my life.  
  • Host: Ha, seriously. But the flip side is, if you're kind to them, they actually pay it back — dropping little shiny gifts at your feet.  
  • Guest: That's so sweet, actually. Like a tiny feathered thank-you card.  
  • Host: And it's not just for show either — it's real reciprocity. They remember faces for years.  
  • Guest: Wait, so if I'm rude to a crow in the park, it's going to hold it against me forever?  
  • Host: Basically, yeah. There's research showing crows can even warn other crows about a specific person.  
  • Guest: Okay, I am officially going to watch my step around crows from now on.  
  • Host: Smart move. They trick each other too — pretending to hide food in one spot while secretly stashing it somewhere else.  
  • Guest: So they're basically playing mind games with each other over snacks.  
  • Host: Pretty much. They've even figured out cause and effect — pulling up a string with their feet to get food dangling on the end.  
  • Guest: That's next-level problem solving for something with a brain the size of a walnut.  
  • Host: Right, it really turns everything on its head — this idea that a big brain is the only way to be smart.  
  • Guest: I'll never look at a crow out of the corner of my eye the same way again.  
  • Host: Ha, good, because apparently they're keeping tabs on us too.  
  • Guest: Well, in that case, I'm going to be extra polite to every bird I see, just in case.  
  • Host: Probably a smart move. You never know who's on the guest list for the neighborhood grudge board.  
  • Guest: That's it for today then — stay on the crows' good side, everyone!
