Vocabulary Study Guide

Click on each word to see its definition and example

die-hard
Definition: (adjective, informal) Extremely loyal and devoted; refusing to give up support.
Example: As a die-hard fan of the team, he never misses a single game, even in the rain.
exaggeration
Definition: A statement that makes something seem larger, better, or worse than it really is.
Example: Saying the line was a mile long was an exaggeration, but we did wait an hour.
resonates
Definition: Connects with someone's feelings or experiences in a meaningful way.
Example: Her speech about second chances resonates with people who have started over.
puts into words
Definition: To express a thought or feeling clearly in language.
Example: I was so grateful that I could hardly put my thanks into words.
secret sauce
Definition: (idiom) The special quality or method that makes something successful.
Example: Great service is the secret sauce behind that little shop's popularity.
hits home
Definition: (idiom) To affect someone strongly on a personal or emotional level.
Example: The film about loneliness really hits home for anyone living far from family.
reads the room
Definition: (idiom) To sense and understand the mood of a group of people.
Example: A good teacher reads the room and slows down when students look confused.
stood out
Definition: (phrasal verb) Was clearly noticeable or more impressive than the rest.
Example: Among all the entries, her painting stood out for its bright colors.
goes out of her way
Definition: (idiom) Makes a special effort to do something, more than expected.
Example: Our manager goes out of her way to make new employees feel welcome.
going through the motions
Definition: (idiom) Doing something without real interest, effort, or feeling.
Example: He was so tired that he was just going through the motions at practice.
showing up
Definition: (idiom) Being present and giving real effort, especially for others.
Example: Half of being a good friend is just showing up when people need you.
on the same page
Definition: (idiom) In agreement; sharing the same understanding.
Example: Before we start, let's make sure we're all on the same page.
mind-blowing
Definition: (adjective, informal) Extremely surprising, impressive, or exciting.
Example: The view from the top of the mountain was absolutely mind-blowing.
do a double take
Definition: (idiom) To look again in surprise because something is unexpected.
Example: I did a double take when I saw my old teacher working at the cafe.
fixate on
Definition: (phrasal verb) To focus too much attention on one thing.
Example: Try not to fixate on one bad grade; look at your progress over the year.
shared experience
Definition: (noun phrase) An event people go through together, creating a connection.
Example: Surviving the storm became a shared experience that brought the village closer.
bucket-list
Definition: (adjective) Relating to things you want to do or achieve before you die.
Example: Seeing the northern lights has always been at the top of my bucket-list.
bonding
Definition: Forming a close relationship or connection with others.
Example: The team went hiking together as a way of bonding before the new season.
flips the script
Definition: (idiom) Changes a situation completely, often in a surprising way.
Example: The underdog team flipped the script and won the championship.
therapy session
Definition: (noun phrase) A meeting to help someone feel better emotionally; (informal) anything healing.
Example: For me, a long walk in the woods is like a therapy session.
word of mouth
Definition: (idiom) Information or recommendations passed from person to person in talk.
Example: The restaurant became popular mostly through word of mouth.
game changer
Definition: (idiom) Something that significantly changes the way things are done.
Example: The new app was a game changer for how our team shares files.
takeaway
Definition: The main idea or lesson you get from something.
Example: The takeaway from the workshop was to plan ahead and stay flexible.
got to hand it to her
Definition: (idiom) To give someone the credit or praise they deserve.
Example: You have to hand it to her; she finished the race despite the injury.
couldn't agree more
Definition: (idiom) To agree completely with what someone has said.
Example: When she said we needed a vacation, I couldn't agree more.
give it another spin
Definition: (idiom) To try or listen to something again.
Example: I didn't like the song at first, but after giving it another spin, I loved it.
