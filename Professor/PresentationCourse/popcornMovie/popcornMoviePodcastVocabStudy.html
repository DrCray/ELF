Vocabulary Study Guide

Click on each word to see its definition and example

came across
Definition: (phrasal verb) To find or discover something by chance.
Example: While cleaning the attic, I came across a box of old family photographs.
hooked
Definition: (adjective, informal) Very interested in or unable to stop doing something.
Example: After just one episode, I was completely hooked on the show.
no-brainer
Definition: (noun, informal) A decision or choice that is very easy and obvious.
Example: With free delivery and a lower price, ordering online was a no-brainer.
catch on
Definition: (phrasal verb) To become popular or widely used.
Example: New slang sometimes takes years to catch on with older generations.
set the stage
Definition: (idiom) To prepare the conditions for something to happen later.
Example: The opening scene set the stage for the drama that followed.
tough times
Definition: (noun phrase) A difficult period, especially financially.
Example: During tough times, families often support one another more than usual.
cash in on
Definition: (idiom) To make money from something, often by taking advantage of a situation.
Example: The company tried to cash in on the popularity of the new trend.
win-win
Definition: (adjective/noun) A situation that is good or beneficial for everyone involved.
Example: Carpooling is a win-win: you save money and you help the environment.
mouthwatering
Definition: (adjective) Looking or smelling so delicious that it makes you want to eat.
Example: The bakery window was full of mouthwatering cakes and pastries.
kick in
Definition: (phrasal verb) To start to take effect or become active.
Example: It usually takes about twenty minutes for the medicine to kick in.
up a notch
Definition: (idiom) To a higher level of quality or intensity.
Example: Adding fresh herbs really takes a simple soup up a notch.
tied together
Definition: (phrasal verb) Connected or linked so that things form a single whole.
Example: All the chapters are tied together by one central character.
on autopilot
Definition: (idiom) Doing something automatically, without thinking about it.
Example: I was so tired that I made my morning coffee on autopilot.
die-hard
Definition: (adjective) Extremely loyal or devoted to something, refusing to give up.
Example: Even in freezing rain, the die-hard fans stayed to watch the whole game.
second nature
Definition: (idiom) Something so familiar that you do it easily and without effort.
Example: After years of driving, changing gears becomes second nature.
game changer
Definition: (noun) Something that dramatically changes a situation or the way things are done.
Example: The smartphone was a real game changer for the way we communicate.
cozy
Definition: (adjective) Warm, comfortable, and pleasant.
Example: We spent the rainy afternoon in a cozy café with warm drinks.
the best of both worlds
Definition: (idiom) A situation where you enjoy the advantages of two different things at once.
Example: Working from home two days a week gives her the best of both worlds.
pass down
Definition: (phrasal verb) To give or teach something to younger generations.
Example: Many families pass down recipes from one generation to the next.
stand the test of time
Definition: (idiom) To remain popular, useful, or valued for a very long time.
Example: True classics are the books that stand the test of time.
go all out
Definition: (idiom) To make the greatest possible effort.
Example: When they host dinner parties, they always go all out with the menu.
hit the spot
Definition: (idiom) To be exactly what is needed or wanted, especially food or drink.
Example: A warm bowl of soup can really hit the spot on a cold evening.
in a nutshell
Definition: (idiom) In a few words; briefly and simply.
Example: The whole proposal, in a nutshell, comes down to saving more and spending less.
fall back on
Definition: (phrasal verb) To rely on something or someone when other options fail.
Example: If the business fails, she has her teaching degree to fall back on.
roots
Definition: (noun) The origin or source of something.
Example: The festival has its roots in an ancient harvest celebration.
lure
Definition: (verb) To attract or tempt someone, often toward something.
Example: Bright advertisements are designed to lure shoppers into the store.
