Vocabulary Study Guide

Click on each word to see its definition and example

no-brainer
Definition: (idiom) Something so obviously true, easy, or sensible that it requires little thought.
Example: Choosing the free upgrade was a no-brainer.
blows my mind
Definition: (idiom) Describes something so surprising or impressive that it's hard to believe.
Example: The size of the ancient pyramids just blows my mind.
break the bank
Definition: (idiom) To cost an extremely large amount of money.
Example: You can eat well in that city without needing to break the bank.
falling apart
Definition: (idiom/phrase) In very poor condition; about to stop working or break completely.
Example: My old laptop is falling apart, so I finally bought a new one.
give it a shot
Definition: (idiom) To attempt something, especially without being sure of the result.
Example: I've never painted before, but I'll give it a shot.
turn on a dime
Definition: (idiom) To change direction or situation very suddenly and completely.
Example: The stock market can turn on a dime, so investors need to stay alert.
dig deep
Definition: (idiom) To make a great physical or mental effort, especially when things are difficult.
Example: The runners had to dig deep to finish the final mile of the marathon.
off the charts
Definition: (idiom) Extremely high or impressive; far beyond normal levels.
Example: Ticket demand for the concert was off the charts within minutes.
on the same page
Definition: (idiom) In agreement; understanding something the same way as someone else.
Example: Before the meeting, the team made sure everyone was on the same page.
