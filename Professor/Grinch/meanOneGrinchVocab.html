Mean One, Mr. Grinch
Vocabulary

  • 1. Heel (a person)

    Someone who is mean and unfair. They might lie, cheat, or hurt others. ️‍

    The man yelled at his wife in front of everyone; What a real heel!

  • 2. Cuddly adj (cuddle verb)

    Soft and huggable, making you feel warm and safe.

    My teddy bear is so cuddly. I cuddle him when I sleep at nights.

  • 3. Charming

    Very nice and pleasant, making you want to be friends with them.

    The waiter greeted us with a charming smile and made us feel welcome.

  • 4. Greasy

    Slippery and oily, like something covered in fat. ️‍

    After I fried the bacon, the pan was greasy and hard to clean.

  • 5. Vile

    Very disgusting and unpleasant, almost like something poisonous.

    The old trash can smelled vile, so I held my breath as I threw it away.

  • 6. Foul

    Having a very bad smell, like rotten food or dirty water.

    The dirty socks in the hamper smelled so foul that I had to open the window.

  • 7. Nasty

    Unpleasant and dirty, making you want to stay away. ️‍

    The spilled yogurt on the floor looked nasty, so I grabbed a mop to clean it up.

  • 8. Gunk

    Sticky and gooey dirt or grime, often hard to remove.

    The gunk around the stovetop needed a good scrubbing with cleaning spray.

  • 9. Sot

    Someone who drinks too much alcohol often and behaves poorly.

    The sot stumbled down the street, muttering to himself after spending all day in the pub.

  • 10. Splotched

    Covered in uneven patches of dirt or stains.

    My new white shirt got splotched with dirt and mud after I fell in the garden.

  • 11. Moldy spots

    Green or black fuzzy patches that grow on food or damp surfaces.

    The old bread had moldy spots, so I threw it away and bought fresh bread.

  • 12. Appalling

    So bad or unpleasant that it shocks you.

    The condition of the house was appalling—dirty dishes, dirty clothes, moldy walls, and muddy floors..

  • 13. Overflowing

    Too full, spilling out over the edges.

    The bathtub was overflowing because I forgot to turn off the water.

  • 14. Disgraceful

    So bad or embarrassing that it makes you feel ashamed.

    The litterbugs left mountains of rubbish in the park, which was disgraceful behavior.

  • 15. Assortment

    A mixed collection of different things.

    The bakery had an assortment of delicious cakes and pastries to choose from.

  • 16. Deplorable

    Very bad and unacceptable, making you feel angry or upset.

    The job had deplorable working conditions – no breaks, short lunches, long hours – and with no air conditioning, it felt like a sauna inside.

  • 17. Rubbish

    Trash or garbage that you throw away.

    We took out the rubbish after dinner and put it in the bin outside.

  • 18. Mangled

    Twisted and bent out of shape, often broken or damaged.

    The car was mangled in the accident, with its doors hanging open.

  • 19. Tangled

    Twisted and knotted together, difficult to separate.

    My headphones were tangled up in my bag, so I had to carefully untangle them before using them.

  • 20. Knots

    Lumps or bumps caused by something being tightly tied or twisted.

    The bows on my shoelaces had become knots; I couldn’t untie them.
