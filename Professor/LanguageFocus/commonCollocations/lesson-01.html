Asking & Answering

Lesson 1  ·  General Communication

B1 – B2

Register Key

General Casual News Professional Workplace
4 phrases  ·  5 example sentences each  ·  See how each phrase is used across different real-world contexts.

1 ask a question
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆If you don't understand something, don't be afraid to ask a question.General
  • ◆Hey, can I ask you a question real quick?Casual
  • ◆The reporter stopped the official to ask a question about the new policy.News
  • ◆Employees are encouraged to ask a question during the Q&A session.Professional
  • ◆The supervisor paused the demonstration so workers could ask a question.Workplace
2 answer a question
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆She took a moment to think before she answered the question.General
  • ◆I'll answer your question, but give me a second to think.Casual
  • ◆The minister refused to answer a question about the budget cuts.News
  • ◆The candidate was well-prepared and able to answer every question confidently.Professional
  • ◆The technician answered the question clearly so the whole team understood.Workplace
3 ask for clarification
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆If instructions are unclear, it's always better to ask for clarification.General
  • ◆Wait — I need to ask for clarification. What do you mean exactly?Casual
  • ◆Several lawmakers asked for clarification on the wording of the new bill.News
  • ◆Before proceeding, the consultant asked for clarification on the project scope.Professional
  • ◆The new employee asked for clarification before starting the task.Workplace
4 respond to a question
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆He responded to every question with patience and detail.General
  • ◆She didn't really respond to my question — she just changed the subject.Casual
  • ◆The CEO responded to questions about the company's financial losses.News
  • ◆The team leader responded to each question raised during the briefing.Professional
  • ◆Staff are expected to respond to questions from customers promptly.Workplace

raise a concern
