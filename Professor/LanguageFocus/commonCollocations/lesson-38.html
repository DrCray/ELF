Reports & Findings

Lesson 38  ·  Professional & Workplace

B1 – B2

Register Key

General Casual News Professional Workplace
4 phrases  ·  5 example sentences each  ·  See how each phrase is used across different real-world contexts.

1 write a report
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Writing a clear, well-organized report is a skill that serves you in almost any career.General
  • ◆I have to write a report by Friday and I haven't even started yet.Casual
  • ◆The journalist spent three months investigating before writing her report on corporate corruption.News
  • ◆All project managers are required to write a report at the end of each phase.Professional
  • ◆After an incident, the staff member involved must write a report and submit it within 24 hours.Workplace
2 publish a report
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Publishing a report is one of the most effective ways to raise awareness about an issue.General
  • ◆They finally published the report — only took two years.Casual
  • ◆The independent commission published a report highly critical of the government's response.News
  • ◆The research department published a report outlining its findings from the two-year study.Professional
  • ◆The health and safety committee publishes a quarterly report on all incidents and near-misses.Workplace
3 present findings
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Presenting your findings clearly is just as important as the quality of the research itself.General
  • ◆I'm nervous about presenting my findings — what if people push back on the conclusions?Casual
  • ◆The lead scientist presented the team's findings at an international conference in Vienna.News
  • ◆Each team was given fifteen minutes to present its findings to the executive committee.Professional
  • ◆The quality assurance team presented its findings at the monthly operations review.Workplace
4 confirm a report
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Always wait for an official source to confirm a report before acting on it.General
  • ◆Has anyone actually confirmed the report, or is it still just a rumor at this point?Casual
  • ◆The defense ministry confirmed the report of military activity near the northern border.News
  • ◆The findings were independently verified before the organization confirmed the report publicly.Professional
  • ◆The site manager confirmed the report of the near-miss incident and launched a full review.Workplace
★ Bonus

back up a claim

  • ◆Strong writing always backs up its claims with specific evidence rather than general statements.General
  • ◆Before presenting to the board, make sure every claim in your report is backed up with solid data.Professional
