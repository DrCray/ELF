Collocation Mini-Lessons

Giving Advice & Feedback

Lesson 9  ·  General Communication

B1 – B2

Register Key

General Casual News Professional Workplace
4 phrases  ·  5 example sentences each  ·  See how each phrase is used across different real-world contexts.

1 give advice
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆A good friend gives honest advice, even when it's hard to hear.General
  • ◆Can I give you some advice? Don't send that message tonight.Casual
  • ◆The agency gives advice to small businesses on how to access government grants.News
  • ◆The consultant was brought in to give advice on restructuring the supply chain.Professional
  • ◆Experienced staff are expected to give advice to newer team members when needed.Workplace
2 offer advice
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆She offered advice without being asked, and it actually helped.General
  • ◆I'm not trying to tell you what to do — I'm just offering some advice.Casual
  • ◆The WHO offered advice to governments on managing the early stages of the outbreak.News
  • ◆The senior partner offered advice on how to handle the difficult negotiation.Professional
  • ◆The health and safety officer offered advice on proper lifting techniques.Workplace
3 give feedback
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Honest feedback helps people grow, even when it stings a little.General
  • ◆I'll give you feedback after I've had a chance to read the whole thing.Casual
  • ◆The panel gave feedback to each of the finalists after the competition.News
  • ◆Managers are expected to give feedback to their team members at least once a quarter.Professional
  • ◆The supervisor gave feedback on the team's output at the end of each shift.Workplace
4 offer help
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆When someone is struggling, offering help can make a real difference.General
  • ◆Hey, do you need a hand? I'm happy to offer some help.Casual
  • ◆The neighboring country offered help in the form of emergency medical supplies.News
  • ◆The senior analyst offered help to the new recruit when she was stuck on the model.Professional
  • ◆If a colleague is falling behind, don't hesitate to offer help where you can.Workplace
★ Bonus

take on board

  • ◆She took the criticism on board and revised the report before the deadline.Professional
  • ◆It's important to take feedback on board rather than getting defensive about it.General
