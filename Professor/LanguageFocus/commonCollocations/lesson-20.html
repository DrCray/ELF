Acting & Initiating

Lesson 20  ·  Professional & Workplace

B1 – B2

Register Key

General Casual News Professional Workplace
4 phrases  ·  5 example sentences each  ·  See how each phrase is used across different real-world contexts.

1 take action
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Knowing about a problem and taking action to fix it are two very different things.General
  • ◆Someone needs to take action here — this has been going on too long.Casual
  • ◆The government was urged to take action after the third major flooding event in a year.News
  • ◆The compliance team took action immediately after the audit revealed the irregularities.Professional
  • ◆The site manager took action as soon as the safety violation was reported.Workplace
2 take steps
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Taking small steps in the right direction every day adds up over time.General
  • ◆You have to take some steps to fix this — it won't sort itself out.Casual
  • ◆The administration took steps to strengthen border security following the incident.News
  • ◆The company took steps to improve data security after a minor breach was detected.Professional
  • ◆Management took steps to address the ventilation problem before it became a health issue.Workplace
3 take initiative
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆People who take initiative tend to stand out in any environment.General
  • ◆Just take the initiative — don't wait for someone to tell you what to do.Casual
  • ◆A group of local residents took the initiative to clean up the polluted riverbank themselves.News
  • ◆The junior associate took the initiative to prepare a full briefing before being asked.Professional
  • ◆She took the initiative to reorganize the stockroom during a slow period, and the manager noticed.Workplace
4 make an effort
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Good relationships don't happen on their own — you have to make an effort.General
  • ◆Can you at least make an effort? You haven't even tried yet.Casual
  • ◆The two countries made a joint effort to address illegal fishing in shared waters.News
  • ◆The team made a real effort to meet the revised deadline despite the added pressure.Professional
  • ◆Staff are expected to make an effort to keep the work area clean throughout the shift.Workplace
★ Bonus

go the extra mile

  • ◆Customers remember the people who go the extra mile to help them.Workplace
  • ◆She always goes the extra mile, and it shows in the quality of everything she produces.General
