Risk & Opportunity

Lesson 14  ·  General Communication

B1 – B2

Register Key

General Casual News Professional Workplace
4 phrases  ·  5 example sentences each  ·  See how each phrase is used across different real-world contexts.

1 take a chance
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Sometimes you have to take a chance on something you believe in.General
  • ◆Just take a chance — the worst they can say is no.Casual
  • ◆The young entrepreneur took a chance on an untested business model and succeeded.News
  • ◆The company took a chance on an unproven technology that ultimately paid off.Professional
  • ◆The team leader took a chance on a new scheduling method that improved output significantly.Workplace
2 take a risk
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Every worthwhile decision involves taking some kind of risk.General
  • ◆I don't know — taking that risk seems like a bad idea right now.Casual
  • ◆The agency warned that taking risks with public health could have severe consequences.News
  • ◆Investors are being advised not to take unnecessary risks in the current market.Professional
  • ◆Never take a risk with safety procedures, no matter how small the task seems.Workplace
3 have a chance
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆Not everyone has a chance to study abroad — make the most of it if you do.General
  • ◆Did you have a chance to read what I sent you?Casual
  • ◆The challenger had a chance to speak directly to voters during the televised debate.News
  • ◆All team members will have a chance to contribute to the new proposal.Professional
  • ◆Every employee will have a chance to complete the updated safety training by Friday.Workplace
4 get a chance
Example Sentences ▾
  • ◆I hope you get a chance to visit — it's a beautiful city.General
  • ◆Let me know when you get a chance to look it over.Casual
  • ◆The local team rarely gets a chance to compete at the international level.News
  • ◆New analysts will get a chance to present their findings at the quarterly review.Professional
  • ◆All new staff get a chance to shadow an experienced colleague during their first week.Workplace
★ Bonus

seize an opportunity

  • ◆When a good opportunity comes along, don't hesitate — seize it.General
  • ◆The company seized the opportunity to expand into the market before its competitors did.Professional
