Everyday Spoken English

Common Expressions for Conversational English

01See it/this through
To continue with something until it is finished, even if it becomes difficult.
I know the project has been harder than expected, but I want to see it through. A: Are you still thinking about quitting the course? B: No, I've decided to see it through. I'm too close to the end to stop now.
02Keep track of
To stay informed about something, or to record information so that it is not forgotten or lost.
It's hard to keep track of all the changes they've made to the schedule. She uses a spreadsheet to keep track of her monthly expenses.
03Break new ground
To do something that has not been done before; to introduce a new idea, method, or development in a particular area.
The research team broke new ground with their approach to treating the disease. A: Is the film worth watching? B: It doesn't exactly break new ground, but it's well made and genuinely entertaining.
