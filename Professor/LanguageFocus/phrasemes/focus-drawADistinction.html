Everyday Spoken English

Common Expressions for Conversational English

01Draw a distinction
To clearly identify and explain the difference between two things.
A: So you're saying they're the same? B: Not exactly — I'd draw a distinction between being busy and being productive.
02Bear the consequences
To accept and deal with the results of a decision or action, especially negative ones.
You made the call without consulting anyone, so you'll have to bear the consequences. A: What happens if the plan doesn't work? B: Then whoever approved it will have to bear the consequences.
03Strike a balance
To find a point that fairly considers two competing needs or demands.
It's hard to strike a balance between giving feedback and not damaging someone's confidence. A: How do you manage work and family time? B: Honestly, I'm still trying to strike a balance.
04Lose track of something
To fail to stay informed or aware of something over time; to no longer know the current state of something.
With everything going on, I completely lost track of where that project stands. A: Do you know how many hours we've put into this? B: I've totally lost track — it feels like weeks.
