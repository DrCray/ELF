📰 Overview

Learn the difference between advice and advise with clear explanations, pronunciation tips, practical grammar rules, and natural example sentences. This lesson focuses on one of the most commonly confused words in English and shows how to use each word correctly in everyday and professional situations.

🎯 The Core Contrast

ADVICE = noun — a suggestion or recommendation (the thing itself)  |  ADVISE = verb — the action of giving advice

🔊 Pronunciation — this is key!

adVICE /ədˈvaɪs/ → ends with /s/ sound (like "ice")   |   adVISE /ədˈvaɪz/ → ends with /z/ sound (like "eyes")

ADVICE 📘 noun · uncountable · the suggestion
A noun — the recommendation or suggestion itself. It is uncountable: no plural form, and no "an" before it. Use "some advice" or "a piece of advice."
📌 some advice · a piece of advice · NOT "an advice" / "advices"
📖 Example: "She gave me some very useful advice about how to prepare for the interview — I followed it and got the job."
📖 Example: "He ignored the doctor's advice to rest and went straight back to work — two weeks later, he was worse than before."
📖 Example: "If you need advice on your visa application, the embassy website has a detailed guide."
ADVISE 🗣️ verb · the action of giving advice
A verb — the action of telling someone what you think they should do. More formal than "recommend" in many contexts.
📌 advise + someone + to (verb) | advise + (that) clause
📖 Example: "The lawyer advised her client to say nothing until they had reviewed all the evidence together."
📖 Example: "The health authority advised the public to stay indoors during the storm and avoid travelling unless absolutely necessary."
📖 Example: "I would advise you to read the contract very carefully before signing — there are clauses that could cost you later."
🎯 Usage Guidelines practical map + examples
💡 Note: These are guidelines to get you started, not strict rules.
📘 Use ADVICE (noun) when...
you are referring to the suggestion itself — the content of what someone recommends. Remember: no plural, no "an."

📘 "The best advice she ever received was to always get a second opinion before making a major financial decision."

📘 "Do you have any advice for someone starting their first management role? I feel completely unprepared."

🗣️ Use ADVISE (verb) when...
you want to describe the action of giving advice — someone is actively recommending something to someone else.

📘 "Doctors advise patients to reduce salt intake if they have high blood pressure — it can make a significant difference over time."

📘 "I would strongly advise against signing anything before speaking to a lawyer."

✨ Quick rule: Can you use it as a thing you "give" or "receive"? → advice (noun). Is it something you "do" to someone? → advise (verb).
