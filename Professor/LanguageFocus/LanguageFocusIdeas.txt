### 1. Phrasal Verbs: Movement & Social Interaction
- **Run into / Bump into** — Meet someone unexpectedly
- **Hang out (with)** — Spend casual time together
- **Meet up (with)** — Arrange to see someone
- **Drop in / Drop by / Stop by** — Visit casually or unexpectedly
- **Catch up (with / on)** — Meet to exchange news / get up to date
- **Reach out (to)** — Contact someone (often for help/connection)
- **Show up / Turn up** — Arrive / appear (often unexpectedly)
- **Get together** — Meet as a group

### 2. Phrasal Verbs: Work, Decisions & Planning
- **Put off** — Postpone / delay; also: discourage someone
- **Figure out** — Understand / solve a problem
- **Take on** — Accept responsibility / hire / challenge
- **Sort out** — Resolve / organize / fix
- **Carry out** — Perform / conduct (task, research, plan)
- **Set up** — Arrange / establish / prepare
- **Work out** — Solve / plan successfully / end well / exercise
- **Come up with** — Invent / suggest an idea or solution
- **Think up** — Invent (often quickly/casually)

### 3. Phrasal Verbs: Problems, Changes & Emotions
- **Break down** — Stop working (machine) / lose emotional control / analyze
- **Get over** — Recover from illness / emotion / move past
- **Give up (on)** — Stop trying / quit a habit
- **Turn down** — Reject / refuse / lower volume
- **Call off** — Cancel
- **Go ahead (with)** — Proceed / give permission to start
- **Fall through** — Fail to happen / be canceled (plans/deals)
- **Come to nothing** — Fail / produce no result
- **Calm down** — Relax when angry/stressed
- **Cheer up** — Become happier

### 4. Phrasal Verbs: Other High-Utility / Everyday
- **Look forward to** (+ gerund) — Feel excited about something future
- **Look into** — Investigate / research
- **Keep up (with)** — Stay at the same level / stay informed
- **Run out (of)** — Use up / finish supply
- **Put up with** — Tolerate / endure
- **Back up** — Support / confirm / make a copy
- **Make up for** — Compensate / balance something negative
- **Live up to** — Meet / achieve expected standards
- **Get away with** — Succeed in wrongdoing without punishment
- **Go out of one's way** — Make extra effort to help
- **Step up** — Increase effort / take responsibility
- **Cut corners** — Do something cheaply/easier (often badly)

### 5. Phrasal Verbs: Result / Ending / Situation
- **Turn out (that)** — Result / happen in the end (often surprising)
- **End up** — Finish or arrive in a situation (unexpectedly/as a result)
- **Wind up** — End up / finish in a situation (informal)
- **Come out** — Be revealed / result (e.g., photos, truth)

### 6. Commonly Confused Words & Pairs
- **Affect** (verb) vs. **Effect** (noun/verb)
- **Hope** vs. **Wish**
- **Imply** vs. **Infer**
- **Accountable** vs. **Responsible**
- **Quite** vs. **Pretty** vs. **Rather** (as intensifiers: very)
- **Travel** vs. **Trip** vs. **Journey**
- **Win** vs. **Beat**
- **See** vs. **Look** vs. **Watch**
- **Wonder** vs. **Wander**
- **Bring** vs. **Take**
- **Its** vs. **It's** vs. **Its'**
- **Lie** vs. **Lay** (including past tenses: lay/lain vs. laid)
- **Rise** vs. **Raise** vs. **Arise**
- **Advice** (noun) vs. **Advise** (verb)

### 7. Multi-Meaning Words (Polysemy)

- **Take** — Get and carry away / take - a point of view / etc.
- **Spin** — Move in circles fast / one's version of a thing
- **Bear** — Carry/endure / animal / produce (fruit) / "bear with me"
- **Light** — Not heavy / illumination / ignite / not dark
- **Turn on / Turn off / Turn up / Turn down** — Devices, volume, reject
- **Put on / Take off** — Wear/remove clothes; plane depart
- **Pick up / Put down** — Lift/collect vs. place down
- **Wake up** vs. **Get up** — Stop sleeping vs. leave bed & start day

### 8. Prepositions & Location/Time
- **In / On / At** — For places and times (enclosed/month vs. surface/day vs. point/time)
- **Tied to / Tied up with** — Closely linked / connected
- **Linked to** — Connected (often evidence-based)
- **Related to** — Connected in some way
- **Have to do with** — Be connected/related
- **Stem from** — Originate / be caused by
- **Boil down to** — Reduce to the essential point

### 9. Causation & Outcome Expressions
- **Lead to** vs. **Result in** vs. **Bring about**
- **In the long run** — Over a long period / eventually
- **On the whole** — Generally / overall
- **At the end of the day** — Ultimately / when everything is considered
- **As far as … is concerned / goes** — Regarding / in my opinion

### 10. Other Useful / Everyday Expressions
- **Table a discussion** — US (postpone) vs. UK/Aus (bring up for discussion)
- **Look for** vs. **Find out** — Search actively vs. discover information
- **Go on / Carry on** — Continue / happen
- **Come back** vs. **Get back** — Return here vs. return (general) / recover
- **Hold on / Hang tight** — Wait / be patient
- **Pass away / Pass on / Pass out** — Euphemisms for death / fainting / sharing
- **Make up** — Invent / reconcile / cosmetics
- **Lose track of** — Forget about / no longer follow
