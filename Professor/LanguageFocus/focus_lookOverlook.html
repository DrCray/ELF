Look Over vs. Overlook

Look Over

Meaning: To examine or review something.

Can you look over my essay for any mistakes?

She asked her colleague to look over the contract before signing.

He looked over the menu carefully before ordering.

Overlook

Meaning: To fail to notice or consider something; to ignore.

It’s easy to overlook small details when you’re in a hurry.

She overlooked the typo in her report.

Don’t overlook the importance of regular exercise.

Bonus: Come Over & Overcome

Come Over: Why don’t you come over for dinner tonight?

Overcome: She worked hard to overcome her fear of public speaking.
