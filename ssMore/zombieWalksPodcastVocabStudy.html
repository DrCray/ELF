ragged
Definition: (adjective) Old, torn, and in bad condition; wearing old or torn clothes.
Example: After the storm, the refugees arrived at the shelter wearing ragged clothes and looking exhausted.
chaos
Definition: (noun) A situation where there is no order or control; complete confusion.
Example: The teacher tried to maintain order, but the classroom descended into chaos when the fire alarm went off.
creativity
Definition: (noun) The ability to make or think of new and original ideas or things.
Example: The art competition encouraged students to show their creativity by designing original sculptures.
doubles as
Definition: (phrasal verb) To have a second purpose or function; to serve two different roles at the same time.

dig in
Definition: (phrasal verb) To search deeply or carefully for information; to investigate something thoroughly.
Example: If you dig in to the history of this building, you'll find some fascinating stories about the original owners.
evolved
Definition: (verb, past tense) Changed and developed slowly over time; became more advanced or different.
Example: The technology industry has evolved dramatically over the past twenty years.
dedicated
Definition: (adjective) Designed for or used for only one particular purpose; reserved exclusively for something.
Example: The library has a dedicated room for group study sessions that can't be used for anything else.
promo
Definition: (noun) Short for "promotion" or "promotional"; a short video or advertisement used to advertise something.
Example: The movie studio released a short promo video to generate excitement before the film's premiere.
spin
Definition: (noun) A particular way of presenting or interpreting something; a unique twist or version.
Example: The restaurant puts its own unique spin on traditional Italian recipes by adding local ingredients.
vibe
Definition: (noun, informal) The feeling or atmosphere of a place or situation; the mood that you sense.
Example: The beach resort has a relaxed vibe that makes visitors feel instantly at ease.
tied to
Definition: (phrasal verb) Connected to or related to something; caused by or dependent on something.
Example: Many environmental problems are tied to overconsumption and waste.
social action
Definition: (noun phrase) Programs or activities designed to help people or improve society; organized efforts to create positive change.
Example: The university increased funding for scholarships and other social action programs.
elaborate
Definition: (adjective) Very detailed and complicated; having many carefully planned parts or decorations.
Example: The wedding reception featured elaborate decorations with ice sculptures and crystal chandeliers.
full-on
Definition: (adjective, informal) Complete and total; not holding anything back; at maximum intensity.
Example: Planning a successful business launch requires full-on commitment and long hours.
draw
Definition: (verb) To attract or pull people to a place; to bring in or appeal to people.
Example: The new shopping mall will draw customers from neighboring towns.
special effects
Definition: (noun phrase) Techniques used in movies or shows to create scenes that cannot be done in real life; visual or sound tricks.
Example: Movie makeup artists use special effects to create realistic injuries and aging.
daily grind
Definition: (noun phrase, informal) The boring, repetitive routine of everyday life, especially work; the regular, tiring tasks you do every day.
Example: Many people exercise after work to escape the daily grind of office routine.
angle
Definition: (noun) A particular way of looking at or thinking about something; a point of view or perspective.
Example: From a financial angle, investing in renewable energy makes sense for long-term savings.
underrated
Definition: (adjective) Not given enough recognition or appreciation; better or more important than people think.
Example: The benefits of meditation are underrated by people who've never tried it.
cross paths
Definition: (idiom) To meet someone by chance; to encounter the same person in different places or situations.
Example: During my travels through Southeast Asia, I would often cross paths with the same backpackers in different cities.
bound
Definition: (adjective) Certain or very likely to happen; destined or sure to do something.
Example: With the new highway opening, traffic congestion is bound to decrease significantly.
learning curve
Definition: (noun phrase) The time and process needed to learn a new skill or adapt to something new; the rate at which you learn something.
Example: Starting a new job involves a learning curve as you adapt to different procedures.
comfy
Definition: (adjective, informal) Short for "comfortable"; pleasant to wear or use; making you feel relaxed.
Example: Pack comfy shoes for the hiking trip because we'll be walking for several hours.
oversized
Definition: (adjective) Larger than the normal or usual size; too big.
Example: He bought an oversized jacket thinking it would shrink in the wash.
aftermath
Definition: (noun) The period or situation that comes after a bad or important event; the results or consequences of something.
Example: The aftermath of the hurricane left thousands of homes without power.
underestimate
Definition: (verb) To think something is smaller, less important, or easier than it really is; to not fully recognize the true size or difficulty.
Example: Never underestimate how long it takes to drive across the city during rush hour.
takes
Definition: (noun, plural) Different versions or interpretations of the same thing; personal opinions or perspectives.
Example: The photographer shared different takes on the same landscape by shooting at various times of day.
minimalist
Definition: (adjective) Using very few and only the most necessary elements; simple and without decoration.
Example: The architect preferred a minimalist design with clean lines and neutral colors.
horde
Definition: (noun) A very large group or crowd of people; a mass of people moving together.
Example: During the Black Friday sale, a horde of shoppers rushed into the store when it opened.
