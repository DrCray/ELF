Vocabulary Study Guide

Click on each word to see its definition and example

pack my bags
Definition: (idiom) To prepare to go on a trip; to get ready to leave.
Example: As soon as the exam was over, I wanted to pack my bags and head to the beach.
that big of a deal
Definition: (idiom, informal) Used to say that something is more important or larger than expected.
Example: I didn't realize breakfast was that big of a deal at this hotel until I saw the buffet.
takes care of everything
Definition: (idiom) Handles or manages every part of a task so you don't have to worry.
Example: Our tour guide took care of everything, from tickets to transportation.
caught my attention
Definition: (idiom) Something that was noticed or found interesting.
Example: The colorful poster in the window caught my attention as I walked by.
blows my mind
Definition: (idiom, informal) To amaze or surprise someone greatly.
Example: The size of the ancient pyramids absolutely blows my mind.
worth it
Definition: (idiom) Deserving of the time, money, or effort spent.
Example: The hike was exhausting, but the view from the top was definitely worth it.
hop off
Definition: (phrasal verb, informal) To get off a vehicle or leave a group briefly.
Example: We decided to hop off the bus early and walk the rest of the way.
plan every little detail
Definition: (idiom) To organize something very carefully, leaving nothing to chance.
Example: She likes to plan every little detail of her trips months in advance.
build your own day
Definition: (idiom) To design or arrange your schedule exactly as you like.
Example: On vacation, I love being able to build my own day instead of following a fixed itinerary.
up my alley
Definition: (idiom) Suited to one's interests or skills.
Example: A quiet weekend with a good book is right up my alley.
something for everyone
Definition: (idiom) A variety of options that will appeal to different people.
Example: The festival has music, food, and games, so there's something for everyone.
get a break
Definition: (idiom) To have a period of rest from work or responsibility.
Example: After watching the kids all week, she finally got to get a break this weekend.
kicks into gear
Definition: (idiom) To start operating actively or with more energy.
Example: The nightlife in that city really kicks into gear after midnight.
trying my luck
Definition: (idiom) To attempt something that involves chance or risk.
Example: He decided to try his luck at the poker table before dinner.
eat your way around the world
Definition: (idiom) To try many different types of international food.
Example: This food festival lets you eat your way around the world in a single afternoon.
seals the deal
Definition: (idiom) To make something final or certain, often the deciding factor.
Example: The free dessert really sealed the deal for me at that restaurant.
has it all
Definition: (idiom) Possesses everything one could want or need.
Example: This little café really has it all: good coffee, quiet music, and fast Wi-Fi.
sold on the idea
Definition: (idiom) To be convinced that something is a good idea.
Example: At first I wasn't sure about the trip, but now I'm completely sold on the idea.
look into booking
Definition: (phrase) To research or consider making a reservation for something.
Example: We should look into booking that campsite before it fills up for summer.
all in
Definition: (idiom) Fully committed or ready to participate.
Example: When my friends suggested a road trip with no plan, I said I was all in.
