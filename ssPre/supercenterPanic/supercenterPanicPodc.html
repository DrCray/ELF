Episode Script

[Audio Intro: Warm, acoustic guitar theme fades in then fades out]
Sarah: Welcome back, everyone! Today, Maya and I were reading an article about a mom named Agatha who lost her four-year-old, Riley, in a grocery store.
Maya: Oh, Sarah, reading that sent chills down my spine. The store went into total lockdown, made announcements, and thankfully, a customer found Riley by the stuffed animals. But those few minutes? Pure panic.
Sarah: Absolute terror. It immediately took me back to two years ago at a massive theme park with my daughter, Lily. One minute she was right beside me near the carousel, and the next—gone.
Maya: Oh no, Sarah! Did they lock the park down?
Sarah: That’s the scariest part—they can't. Theme parks are way too large and open to do a store-style lockdown. You stand there looking at thousands of people, realizing anyone could walk out with your child. Security was great, but those six minutes felt like six agonizing hours until a staff member spotted her near a cotton candy stand.
Maya: I completely get that helpless feeling. I had a similar nightmare at the airport last year with my son, Leo. We were at the crowded baggage claim. I turned around to grab a heavy suitcase, and when I looked up, Leo had wandered off.
Sarah: An airport! That is terrifying! Did they shut down the terminal?
Maya: No, exactly like your theme park situation! High-traffic public hubs like airports simply can’t lock down for safety and logistical reasons. So your brain immediately jumps to the absolute worst-case scenario—he’s gotten onto a shuttle, or worse, left the building entirely with a stranger.
Sarah: How did you find him?
Maya: Thankfully, another mom noticed him wandering toward the exit doors and stopped him. When I ran over, I just burst into tears and squeezed him so tight, exactly like Agatha did with Riley.
Sarah: It’s that instant shift from sheer, breath-stealing panic to overwhelming gratitude.
Maya: Absolutely. The article mentioned Agatha sat Riley down afterward to teach him about store safety, and it’s such a crucial lesson.
Sarah: Exactly. Whether it’s a grocery store, a theme park, or an airport, we have to teach our kids what to do if they get lost—find a uniform, stay put, and never leave with anyone.
Maya: Amen to that. Hug your little ones tight tonight, moms. Thanks for listening, and we'll see you next week!
[Audio Outro: Acoustic guitar theme fades in]
